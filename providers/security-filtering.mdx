---
title: "Security-filtering DNS providers against malware"
description: "DNS providers that block malware and phishing domains without restricting adult content — a good default upgrade from unfiltered DNS for most users."
---

Security-filtering providers block domains associated with malware, ransomware, and phishing while leaving all other content — including adult sites — fully accessible. This makes them a practical upgrade from a plain unfiltered resolver for most users and devices: you get meaningful threat protection without any content restrictions.

<Tip>
  If you are unsure which category to choose, a security-filtering provider is a reasonable default. You get protection against the most common DNS-based threats without any side effects on legitimate browsing.
</Tip>

## Quad9

**Region:** 🇨🇭 CH — Operated by Quad9 Foundation

Quad9 is a non-profit resolver that aggregates threat intelligence from multiple security vendors to block malware domains in real time. It supports both DoH and DoT and validates DNSSEC.

| DoT hostname | `dns.quad9.net` |

## Quad9 w/ ECS

**Region:** 🇨🇭 CH — Operated by Quad9 Foundation

This variant behaves identically to the standard Quad9 resolver but also supports EDNS Client Subnet (ECS), which can improve geolocation accuracy for CDN-hosted content at the cost of sharing a portion of your IP address with upstream resolvers.
