---
title: "Ad-blocking DNS providers for cleaner browsing"
description: "DNS providers that block ads, trackers, and in some cases malware at the resolver level — no browser extension or separate app required."
---

Ad-blocking DNS providers intercept queries for known ad-serving, tracking, and analytics domains before they ever reach your device. Because filtering happens at the DNS layer, it applies system-wide to every app, not just your browser. The providers on this page block at least ads and tracking; some also block malware or phishing domains.

<Info>
  DNS-level ad-blocking cannot filter inline ads on the same domain as the content (for example, YouTube ads served from `youtube.com`). For those cases you still need a browser extension or a local proxy.
</Info>
