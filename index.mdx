Encrypted DNS provides ready-to-install `.mobileconfig` configuration profiles that enable DNS over HTTPS (DoH) and DNS over TLS (DoT) on iOS and macOS. Instead of sending DNS queries in plain text, these profiles encrypt your DNS traffic — protecting your browsing from eavesdropping and tampering on untrusted networks.

## Get started in three steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a provider">
    Browse the [provider list](/providers/overview) and pick a DNS provider that fits your needs. Cloudflare 1.1.1.1, Google Public DNS, and Quad9 are popular starting points.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the profile">
    Click the **HTTPS** or **TLS** install link for your chosen provider. On iPhone or iPad, open the link in Safari. On Mac, download and double-click the `.mobileconfig` file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install on your device">
    Follow the system prompts to install the configuration profile. On iOS, go to **Settings → General → VPN, DNS & Device Management**. On macOS, go to **System Settings → Privacy & Security → Profiles**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why use encrypted DNS?

Standard DNS queries are sent in plaintext, making them visible to anyone on your network — your ISP, the coffee shop Wi-Fi router, or a malicious actor on a public hotspot. Encrypted DNS prevents this by wrapping DNS queries in HTTPS or TLS encryption.
